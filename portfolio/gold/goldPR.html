Gymwork

Strength. Cardio. Flexibility. Balance

Selecting a Skill

I had already spent some time doing yoga and taekwondo in my Bronze and Silver Awards respectively. I wanted to see what else I could do both in an effort to push myself a little further physically while also being conscious that I may not have the ability to attend club training in person with the demands of my degree. I consulted with my PAL Orla before committing to a particular sport, and she suggested gym work to me. I was happy to put it down for the final leg of my Gaisce journey.

I liked that yoga gave me the ability to improve my flexibility and balance. Taekwondo, as done through club training, was a fantastic social outlet and a great way to let off some steam. Gym work was a little more vague and allowed me the flexibility to do something different each week depending on how my body felt and what equipment I had available. This was really important to me as I could try different forms of exercise in the gym, in my home and in my local area. I was also conscious that my body, like everyone else's, has some limitations. Trying different exercises prevented me from injuring myself via over training in any one area.

Getting Started

I wanted to see how I could improve my physical fitness from all aspects. If you've watched the 2024 Paris Olympics, which finished very recently, two athletes bodies can look very different from each other. I wanted to give myself the excuse to try a mix of sports and exercises. Some of these sports I would've been familiar with prior to embarking on my Gaisce journey and other sports I never had the opportunity to try or the interest in when I was younger.

Some of my exercise would've taken place on foot. Walking is probably the easiest form of exercise for me. If the weather was good during the academic year, I would walk through the city at peak times rather than taking the bus to get my step count up. I also lived very close to the entry point to a greenway during my undergrad; it would take me roughly 1 hour and 20 minutes to complete this loop. I did attempt both running and jogging but due to issues with fallen arches and shin splints, I was forced to slow down to walking pace to prevent injury. I have also tried hiking which I find very enjoyable. I prefer to spend my time hiking with a friend or relative rather than on my own. This is because I would hike in areas where I am very isolated which often do not have phone coverage. I would not recommend anyone, particularly women like myself, hike in remote areas alone. This is also due to the risk of injury as well as the general safety aspect of being alone where no one can see you. One of the really great benefits of walking, in addition to the fresh air, is clearing your mind or getting the chance to catch up on voice notes from friends, music and podcasts. If you have a subscription to an audiobook service then this is a good chance to get some reading in too.

I wanted to remain as flexible as possible. I continued doing a certain amount of yoga which was mixed in with taekwondo and Pilates, the latter of which I hadn't tried before. I definitely have yoga poses which I prefer to do such as child's pose, cobra pose and cat pose. Crow pose is definitely one that I struggle with. This involves placing your palms flat on the mat, bending your elbows slightly, allowing your arms hold the entirety of your weight. Your head is parallel to the floor and your legs are resting on your upper arms. If crow pose turns you off yoga then might I suggest trying the easier corpse pose? Flexibility is probably an underrated part of martial arts. We often think of different forms of martial arts as being good for self defence whilst forgetting how much you need to stretch yourself to get that kick up higher and prevent an injury when punching. I definitely found some overlap with the beginners Pilates lessons I watched online and yoga. I think that yoga is the more beginner-friendly of the two exercises. Pilates is a more intense workout for your core and focuses more on building muscle rather than stretching through everything like yoga does.

I tried to improve my cardiovascular fitness too. I tried my hand at following some online dance videos. Even though they were beginner dance videos, I wasn't very good at dance. I found it awkward to follow directions when I couldn't see the screen at all times. I also struggled with the amount of space in my bedroom and accidentally kicked my bed a few times. I had better luck with kickboxing. It was similar enough to taekwondo to feel familiar while still having a slightly different focus. I didn't really enjoy my attempts at HIIT (high intensity interval training). I think that part of this was the fact that it involves a lot of "loud" exercises. I always try to be conscious of others in my house when exercising and I knew that hopping up and down on my bedroom floor would've disturbed my parents too much. I tried some cycling too. We have an exercise bike in my house which I used a handful of times. I found that the bike was much quieter and I liked the ability to adjust the intensity of my bike rides. I used to cycle a bit as a teenager but stopped cycling on the roads after a nasty encounter with a Toyota Avensis. I definitely prefer cycling in the open and enjoy the wind rushing through my hair. I've cycled the full Waterford Greenway and I think that greenways are the best option for people who prefer outdoor cycling.

I didn't really attempt any ball-related sports. I've always been the type to run away from objects moving towards me at high speeds which makes me terrible at the majority of team sports. I tried my hand at GAA as a child, but I never really took to it. In hindsight one of the reasons was that I was separated from the other girls in my class due to the fact that I was slightly younger than them. I wasn't particularly fond of playing basketball when I was in PE classes in secondary school either. Hockey didn't put me up or down either way. Tennis is probably my favourite of any sport with a ball. Our dog loved to play fetch when I was a child, so I would play tennis with him. One of the walls of our house has no windows so it was ideal if I wanted to play tennis alone. The dog was more than delighted to be employed as my ball boy if I hit a shot out of bounds. Tennis is still the sport of all the ones listed in this paragraph that I would feel happiest to try today, even if I have to dig the ball out of the hedge by myself these days.

I did try using equipment in the gym for the first time. I used a rowing machine at the gym. I didn't realise how technical rowing was. I asked a member of staff to show me how to use the rowing machine as I don't think I had even seen one until I was in my 20s. Rowing definitely is a full body workout once you start to get the correct technique down. I'm not strong enough to lift the bar which is usually 20kg or 45lbs. I opted for lifting dumbbells instead. I have bought myself three pairs of dumbbells - 1.5kg, 2kg and 5kg - for the days that I couldn't make it to the gym. I found that I really liked lifting weights even if I get punished the next day. The stiffness is usually the worst the first time you try a new workout video and I found that I wasn't as sore for as long if I repeated the same video that week with the same weights. I think I might make an attempt at using kettlebells in the near future.

I returned to swimming for the first time since I was about 12 years old. Swimming was the only transition year module I failed as I didn't feel comfortable enough to use the changing room in front of my peers. I own two swimsuits, and they're both getting some use throughout the year. I am lucky enough to live within driving distance of multiple beaches, so I can swim in the sea during the summer months. Outside of that I'll use a pool on a rainy day. I think the swimming pool can be a better option if you want to try diving or swim laps. It's definitely harder to judge the distance you've covered in the sea but the salt water is a lot more relaxing than the chlorine of the pool. I've also been able to give my mom a few tips while swimming, such as keeping her arms below the water level when doing the breaststroke. I tend to stick to the breaststroke and the backstroke in the pool, alternating after touching the pool wall after doing a lap. I am more likely to try freestyle swimming in the sea because I'm not counting laps there.

Exercise Today

Overall, as I come to the close of my Gaisce journey, I think I have a much healthier relationship with sport and exercise now than when I began my Bronze Award back in 2021. I certainly have a better relationship with exercise than at any point in my teens when I absolutely loathed my PE classes. I lost half a tooth as a first year student in one of my PE classes which didn't help either. Looking back its made me realise the value of a good teacher (as well as the long-lasting impacts of a terrible one).

While I'm still unlikely to play a sport such as football, I don't think I would've discovered weightlifting if I hadn't bothered trying the Gaisce Award programme. I think the exercise styles that suit me are more slow and controlled in nature. I prefer picking up weights and lifting a little heavier or squeezing out an extra rep to last week. I prefer holding a yoga pose a second longer or stretching a few millimetres more than I would've previously. I went to school with people that really enjoyed team sports, but I'm the type that prefers to compete with my past self rather than against other individuals or teams.

I think I am a little bit more open-minded and interested in sport after this experience. I think I've also learned that I'm not compatible with every sport, the same way that many of my peers from secondary school and I have graduated with completely different degrees. While I enjoyed watching the Irish team place 4th in the women's 4x400m relay, I know that's not something I can do with my legs. I do know that there are sports that I can do (and even enjoy) thanks to moving out of my comfort zone as part of the Gaisce Award. It has also made me realise the value of teams and sportsmanship thanks to the welcoming atmosphere of the taekwondo club in my college.
